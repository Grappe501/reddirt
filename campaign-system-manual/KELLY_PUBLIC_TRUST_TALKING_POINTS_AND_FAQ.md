## 2. Audits and the SBEC 2024 post-election report

- Arkansas **documents** what post-election review includes in **official** SBEC materials. The **2024 General Election Final Post-Election Audit Report** (PDF on the [Arkansas State Board of Election Commissioners](https://sbec.arkansas.gov/) site) is a **primary** read for anyone who wants the **documented** process, not rumor.  
- **Factual handling:** The published 2024 SBEC process includes **hand-counting** selected **voter-marked paper ballots** and **comparing** those results to **machine tabulator** totals, as the report describes. **Do not** quote or paraphrase in campaign copy without **opening the PDF** and aligning **word-for-word** with **counsel** (`ELECTION_CONFIDENCE_TRANSPARENCY_AND_GET_UNDER_THE_HOOD_DOCTRINE.md` §6–7).  
- “I am not asking you to trust a vibe. I am asking you to read the **same public documents** I am reading.”

---

## 3. MIT Election Performance Index (EPI)

- The [MIT Election Data and Science Lab](https://electionlab.mit.edu/) publishes the **Elections Performance Index (EPI)** — a **nonpartisan** benchmark of **election administration** (not a measure of “fraud” or “fraud absence”). **Link** the current EPI page; **do not** imply a state **ranking** is a verdict on **voter** fraud. Use as **context** with Arkansas-specific sources, not a substitute for Arkansas law.

## 4. County clerks, poll workers, transparency, and fixing real problems

- **Clerks / local election officials:** Credit their work. Be explicit where SOS authority **starts and stops** (counsel + `MANUAL_INFORMATION_REQUESTS_FOR_STEVE.md`).  
- **Poll workers:** Honor lawful service — **recruit, train, thank**; do not turn them into a fear narrative (align with `SEGMENTED_MESSAGE_AND_DISTRIBUTION_SOP.md` — **election-confidence** **lane,** not fear content).  
- **Fixing problems:** If a process is broken or confusing, **patch it in public** when possible. **Say plainly** what the SOS can do under statute and what requires the legislature (MI).

## 5. FAQ — voters who worry about fraud

**Q: Do you think Arkansas elections are “rigged”?**  
**A (draft):** “*Rigged* is a heavy word. I work from **evidence**, not drama. I have not seen **credible public evidence** of **systemic** election-administration **fraud** in Arkansas, and I have looked at the public record — **audits,** official **materials,** and **vetted** reporting. That does not mean we never have a **local** problem or a **bad** actor. Those get handled on **facts** and **law** (counsel on any open case). I am for **showing the work** — what checks exist, what paper records exist, and where to read them.”

**Q: What if the machines are wrong?**  
**A (draft):** “Mistakes can happen in any complex system. The test is **paper** where the law provides it, **reconciliation,** and **lawful** **remedies** like **recounts** where the law allows. I would lead with **plain-language** explainers and **transparency,** and I will neither **mock** worries nor **treat** serious allegations as mere social media threads. Counsel and MCE on any specific claim about equipment or a jurisdiction.”

**Q: Why should I trust you?**  
**A (draft):** “You are not asked to trust a black box. I am offering **documentation** you can read and a service ethic built on **showing the work** — that is the job (pair with **bio** and **SOS** **fit** content elsewhere, counsel-reviewed).”

## 6. FAQ — voters who already believe elections are secure

**Q: Is all the fraud talk overblown?**  
**A (draft):** “It is a big state. I separate (a) what **credible** public process review shows, (b) what a **headline** **alleges,** and (c) what a **charge** or **court** **establishes** — and counsel helps keep that line clean. We are not stoking **panic;** we are not sneering at **skeptics** either. The posture is **evidence,** not boredom and not contempt.”

**Q: Why not just say ‘stop worrying’?**  
**A (draft):** “**Dismissiveness** is how you **erode** trust. Voters deserve **adult** **answers** and a map to the **sources** — that is the **‘get under the hood’** promise.”

## 7. Public claim review (before a new line ships on site, ads, or Companion)

1. **Fact-check** to a **primary** (SBEC, SOS, statute) or a **linkable,** **reputable** story — not a meme.  
2. **MCE** + **NDE** (if public).  
3. **Counsel** on contrast, defamation risk, and **alleged**-criminal **references**.  
4. **Log** source URL and **date retrieved** (`CAMPAIGN_COMPANION_ELECTION_QUESTIONS_POLICY.md`, website CMS).  
5. **EPI** ranks and **SBEC** excerpts: **second** reader checks for **misread** risk in paid/earned copy.
