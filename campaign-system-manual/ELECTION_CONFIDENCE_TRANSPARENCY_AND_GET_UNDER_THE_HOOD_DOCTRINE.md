## 1. North Star

**Arkansas elections are run with real safeguards. Trust grows when we explain how things work, invite questions, fix what is broken, and never mock voters’ concerns or treat accountability as optional.**

This is a **“secure, but never dismissive”** posture: confidence without contempt, and transparency without panic.

---

## 2. What to claim (and not claim)

| Intent | Favored framing | Avoid |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| Systemic fraud in Arkansas election **administration** | “I have not seen **credible public evidence of systemic fraud** in how Arkansas **administers** elections — and I have looked at **official** audits, **published** **materials,** and **vetted** reporting. Exact wording: counsel + MCE/NDE before static publish.” | “There is no fraud” / “Fraud is impossible” / shaming people who ask questions |
| Isolated issues | “Elections involve **people and machines** — **isolated** problems, **errors,** and **alleged** misconduct **do** get investigated. The test is **transparent rules,** **evidence,** and **consequences** when something is real.” | Blanket denial that any local story ever matters, or conflating one case with the whole system |
| Voter concerns | “If you’re worried, you deserve **plain-language answers** and a Secretary of State who **shows the work**.” | “Stop worrying” / ridicule / conspiracy-bait |

**Do not** overclaim. **Do not** assert “no fraud ever” in any form. Distinguish **alleged** vs **charged** vs **convicted** with counsel for anything that touches a specific case or person.

---

## 3. Distinguish these topics (do not conflate in one breath)

1. **Election administration** — How ballots are cast, scanned, retained, tabulated, audited, and recounted (lawful process, chain of custody, post-election checks).  
2. **Voter fraud (illegal voting)** — Individual criminal conduct; separate statistics and case law; do not merge with “counting” fears without clarity.  
3. **Petition / canvassing / ballot-access fraud** — Signature, notarization, or process violations in *different* legal lanes than tabulation.  
4. **Isolated local misconduct** — County- or office-level allegations or charges; do not inflate to “the whole state is rigged” or shrink to “nothing matters” without evidence.  
5. **Machine or process error** — Malfunctions, human error, recount outcomes; good SOS answers describe how errors are **detected** and **remedied** under law.  
6. **Public confidence / distrust** — Emotional and informational reality; **core** to “get under the hood,” not a segment to attack.

---

## 4. “Get under the hood” — language bank (adapt with counsel)

- “I’m the kind of **SOS** who **opens the hood,** not **hides** it.”  
- “**Security** isn’t a **black** **box** — I’ll **explain the checks** in language your **neighbors** can repeat at the kitchen table.”  
- “If **something**’s **broke,** I’d **rather** **fix** it **in** the **open** than **pretend** it’s **fine**.”  
- “**Paper** and **procedures** and **people** on the **ground** — **clerks,** **poll** **workers,** and **independent** **post-election** **steps** — I’ll point to **what** the **state** **already** **does,** and what I’d add in **transparency** and **service,** **within** **law**.”

Tie **concrete** **procedural** **claims** to **primary** **sources** (SBEC, SOS) before publishing — see §7.

---

## 5. “Secure, but never dismissive”

- **Secure:** Cite real Arkansas artifacts (e.g. **post-election audit** work described in the SBEC 2024 report) and, where appropriate, **national** administration benchmarks (e.g. **MIT** **Election** **Data** and **Science** **Lab** **Elections** **Performance** **Index** **—** a **process** **measure,** not a fraud verdict).  
- **Never dismissive:** No ridicule of worried voters; no “educate yourself” as a punchline; no rural/elder/tech-poor stereotyping (align with `USER_FRIENDLY_WORKBENCH_UX_REQUIREMENTS.md` accessibility spirit).  
- **Accountable:** When **real** **failures** are in the **news,** the message is: **here is what** **is** **known,** **here is the** **law** **/ **jurisdiction,** **here is the** **remedy** **or** **next** **step** the **SOS** **can** **lawfully** **pursue** — not a blanket “trust us.”

## 6. Audits, paper, clerks, poll workers, transparency (content spine)

- **2024** **Arkansas** **post**-**election** **audit** **(SBEC):** The **State** **Board** of **Election** **Commissioners** **publishes** a **2024** **General** **Election** **Final** **Post**-**Election** **Audit** **Report** (PDF on `https://sbec.arkansas.gov/`) that **describes,** **among** **other** **elements,** **hand**-**counting** **of** **selected** **voter**-**marked** **paper** **ballots** and **comparing** **those** **results** **to** **machine** **tabulator** **totals** **—** **quote** or **paraphrase** **only** **from** **the** **published** **document**; **do** **not** **strengthen** **the** **state’s** **language** in **campaign** **copy** **without** **counsel**.  
- **MIT** **EPI:** **Use** as **a** **nonpartisan** **national** **benchmark** of **election**-**administration** **dimensions,** **not** as proof of any **specific** **fraud** **rate** **or** **as** a **substitute** **for** **Arkansas** **law** **(see** `KELLY_PUBLIC_TRUST_TALKING_POINTS_AND_FAQ.md` **citation** **rules) **.  
- **SOS / SBEC / public security or review materials:** Pull only from official publications; no invented statutory powers (`MANUAL_INFORMATION_REQUESTS_FOR_STEVE.md`).  
- **County clerks; poll workers:** Credit their work; SOS narrative = standards, training, transparency, and service (exact authority split = counsel + MI).  
- **Verified Voting (and similar):** Supplemental context on equipment families and paper-trail ideas — any Arkansas hardware or law claim must come from state sources first (`KELLY_PUBLIC_TRUST_TALKING_POINTS_AND_FAQ.md`).

## 8. What the campaign should never say (add to MCE/NDE guardrails)

- “There is **no** fraud **anywhere,** **ever**” (unprovable overclaim).  
- “**Only** paranoid people care about **election** **security**” (dismissive; harmful).  
- “The **machines** are **always** right — **stop** **asking**” (anti-inquiry; also often false as stated).  
- Unsourced statewide “fraud” percentages, or EPI ranks misread as “fraud proof” or “fraud absence.”  
- Inventing **SOS** **power** to **re-run** **elections,** **unilaterally** **replace** **equipment,** or **ignore** **statute** **(MI** **+** **counsel) **.
