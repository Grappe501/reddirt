## 3. Knowledge domains (illustrative map)

- Arkansas **constitutional** history; **SOS** office history; **election administration** and **ballot initiative** history over time.  
- **County** formation, **local** political **culture** (sourced, not stereotype as fact).  
- Reconstruction, **voting** **rights**, and **civil** **rights** in Arkansas (sensitive; counsel/comms for campaign use).  
- **Women** in Arkansas politics; **labor** and **farming** **history**; **rural/urban** **shifts** (sourced, no invented trend lines).  
- **Winthrop** **Rockefeller** and **two-party** Arkansas **history** (narrative, not a weaponized “history proves” line).  
- **County** **party** **history** and **civic** **institutions,** where **OIS/field** **truth** and **3F** **manuals** allow honest framing.  
- **Libraries,** **archives,** and **civic** **memory;** **Native** **nations** and **pre-statehood** (respectful sourcing; not tokenism).  
- **Black** **political** **history** in Arkansas; **civic** **offices** and **local** **governance** (primary sources; no unsourced “everyone knew” lines).

## 5. Source rules

- **Primary** sources preferred; **Encyclopedia** of Arkansas, **State** **Archives,** **university** and **SOS/official** **state** **materials**; **mark** **oral** **history** as **a** **separate** **provenance** **class**.  
- **No** unsourced myth as fact; no invented quotes; do not use “history proves voters today believe X” (see **Safety** below).

**Safety (non-negotiable):** Do not weaponize history; **distinguish** **analogy** from **empirical** **proof**; no invented **quotes;** no “history **proves**” **a** **modern** **electoral** **outcome**; contrast and hot topics still **MCE+comms+counsel** per `playbooks/`.
