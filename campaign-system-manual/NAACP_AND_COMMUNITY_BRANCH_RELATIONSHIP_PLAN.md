# NAACP and community branch relationship plan (Pass 3E)

**Lane:** `RedDirt/campaign-system-manual` · **Markdown only** · **2026-04-27**  
**Public language:** **Campaign Companion**, **Guided Campaign System**, **Organizing Guide**, **Field Intelligence**, **Message Engine**, **Campaign Operating System**, **Workbench**, **Pathway Guide** — not “AI.”

**Honesty rules:** Do **not** invent **NAACP branch names**, addresses, or officers. If the campaign does not yet have a **verified** list (official source or Steve’s internal list), the **system action** is: **NAACP branch mapping required** — build a **mapping spreadsheet** + **owner** before claiming progress in OIS or public copy. **Relationship ≠ endorsement** and is **not** assumed to be transactional.

---

## 1. Executive summary

NAACP branch and aligned community relationships are **strategic** for **trust**, **civic education**, and **voter protection** alignment. This plan uses a **relationship-first** doctrine, a **branch mapping framework** and **visit ladder** (0–8), **respectful engagement** rules, clear **asks** and **non-asks**, **civic education** alignment, **county** integration, **travel** and **Workbench** hooks, dashboard needs, and a **Steve decision list**. **No** branch list lives in this repo until **verified** or **Steve-approved** for internal use (no PII in examples).

---

## 2. Why NAACP branch relationships matter

- **Election administration** and **voter access** issues overlap with long-standing **civil rights** monitoring and **community** trust.  
- **Listening** and **service** orientation match the campaign’s **non-transactional** community doctrine.  
- **Bridges** to **county** teams, **events**, and **Field Intelligence** without reducing partners to **props**.

---

## 3. Relationship-first doctrine

- **Listen** before **pitch**; document **alignment** and **concerns** — not just “optics.”  
- **No** **assumed** **endorsement** from a **meeting** or **intro**.  
- **Stewardship:** one **relationship** **owner** per branch when possible (see §8 ladder).  
- **Compliance** and **counsel** on any **regulated** or **contrast** content involving partners.

---

## 4. Branch mapping framework

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **A** | **System action (if unmapped):** `NAACP branch mapping required` — assign owner, deadline, data source (official org directories, public chapter pages where permitted, **or** Steve’s list — **not** crowdsourced guesswork in repo). |
| **B** | **Row** per **branch** (when known): name/area served, **county** overlay, **region**, **point of contact** (off-repo), last touch, **ladder** stage. |
| **C** | **Data confidence** Low/Med/High. |
| **D** | **Tie** to `WEEKLY_TRAVEL_AND_EVENT_PROJECTION_SYSTEM.md` **NAACP** **priority** **score** — **targets** to **visit**, not “supporters we invented.” |

---

## 5. Branch visit ladder (0–8)

| Stage | Name |
|-----:|------|
| **0** | **Unknown / not mapped** |
| **1** | **Branch identified** (jurisdiction/area verified) |
| **2** | **Relationship contact identified** |
| **3** | **Introductory meeting requested** |
| **4** | **Meeting held** |
| **5** | **Issue alignment documented** (internal — no public claim without sign-off) |
| **6** | **Joint civic education opportunity identified** (debate, forum, training — governed) |
| **7** | **Volunteer / county bridge established** (handoff to county coordinator) |
| **8** | **Ongoing relationship steward assigned** |

**Rule:** **Stage** **4** does **not** mean **endorsement**. **Stage** **5** is **internal** **alignment** notes, not a **public** list of “supporters.”

---

## 6. Rules for respectful engagement

- **Show up** on **their** **timeline**; **no** last-minute **cancellations** without **apology** and **reschedule**.  
- **Credit** **partners** **accurately** in any **MCE/NDE** or **earned** material.  
- **Do not** **surprise** with **press**; **separate** **comms** **RACI** (strategy manual §16).  
- **Private** or **sensitive** relationships stay **out** of **public** **repo** text.

---

## 7. What we ask for (examples — Steve refines)

- **Mutual** **understanding** of **election** **administration** priorities.  
- **Civic** **education** and **voter** **information** (nonpartisan where required).  
- **Introductions** to **county**-level **civic** leaders when **appropriate**.  
- **Pathway** to **volunteer** roles that **comply** with **age** and **data** **policy**.

---

## 8. What we do not ask for

- **Endorsement** in the first meeting, or **as** a **quid** **pro** **quo**.  
- **PII** or **voter** **file** **access** through partners.  
- **Coordinated** **contrast** **messaging** without **compliance** review.  
- **Filling** the **room** for **staged** **optics** without **value** to the **community**.

---

## 9. Civic education and voter protection alignment

- Frame **SOS** **duties** (access, **transparency**, **integrity** of process) in **factual,** **sourced** language.  
- **Voter** **protection** and **deputy**-level topics: **compliance** and **state** **party** / **legal** where applicable — not invented in the manual.  
- **Message** **Engine** content **suggestions** must pass **comms** + **counsel** for sensitive topics.
