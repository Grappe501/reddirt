# Candidate system training map

**Audience:** Kelly and trusted operators learning the **campaign operating system** through Ask Kelly / Dixie — **not** a substitute for compliance, counsel, or data-class tools.

**Safety:** This document is written to be **admin-safe**: no voter rows, no donor data, no opposition research, no sealed strategy. It **summarizes** navigation and learning order. Deep procedures live in internal manuals and dashboards with proper permissions.

---

## Onboarding stages (aligned with Candidate onboarding UI)

| Stage | Focus |
|-------|--------|
| **Welcome** | Where you are; how this guide relates to dashboards. |
| **How onboarding works** | Pace yourself; skip and return; nothing public changes without saves you confirm elsewhere. |
| **Page content first** | Public wording flows through draft → review → confirm → publish in **Page content**. |
| **Beta feedback** | Queue for tester notes; staff may triage; **you** decide what becomes an edit. |
| **Workbench** | Operational queues and tasks — orientation, not auto-execution from the guide alone. |
| **Voice / Dixie** | Optional; review-first; wake phrase and explicit submit — no publish from speech. |
| **Ready** | You can start Page content or triage feedback on your timing. |

---

## What each dashboard is *for* (high level)

| Area | Purpose (plain language) |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Page content (`/admin/pages`)** | Edit approved **public-facing** pillar copy (hero blocks, etc.) with saves you control. |
| **Ask Kelly beta (`/admin/workbench/ask-kelly-beta`)** | Read structured feedback from testers; prioritize; route real copy work back to Page content when ready. |
| **Workbench (`/admin/workbench`)** | Central place for operational items — calendars, tasks, comms hubs, etc., depending on what the campaign enables. |

The guide **names routes**; it does **not** run campaigns or approvals for you.

---

## What Kelly should learn first

1. **How a public page update travels** — draft vs live, failed saves vs success.
2. **Where tester feedback lands** — and that it **does not** overwrite the site by itself.
3. **Workbench as the map for ongoing ops** — use it with your campaign manager when priorities shift.

---

## What Ask Kelly can explain today

- **Where** approved tasks live (routes, onboarding copy, public-safe excerpts when ingested).
- **How** review-first publishing differs from drafts and suggestions.
- **What** Dixie does and does **not** do (capture → review → you copy onward).

---

## What requires approval (always)

- **Live site copy** — final confirms in Page content / defined admin saves.
- **Financial, compliance, and legal wording** — human and counsel workflows; not delegated to the guide.
- **Data extracts** — voter-file, treasury, granular reports — use the **right dashboard**, not conversational surfaces.

---

## What is **not** available yet (honest boundaries)

| Capability | Status |
|------------|--------|
| Full “Manual of Everything” conversational search over internal manuals | **Staged** — requires curated excerpts + admin-safe retrieval layer. |
| Internet research inside Ask Kelly | **Not** live — see onboarding “Research tools” placeholder. |
| Aggregate reporting / dashboards from conversational chat | Use dedicated reporting tools when provisioned — **planned** integrations, not claiming completion here.
