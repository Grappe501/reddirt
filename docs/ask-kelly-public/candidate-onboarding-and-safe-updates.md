# Candidate onboarding and safe updates

Public-facing help text for **Ask Kelly** on the campaign site. This page describes what visitors and authorized editors can expect— not internal runbooks or data systems.

---

## What can Ask Kelly help me with?

Ask Kelly is the site’s **guide**: it can point you toward the right page, explain how common tasks work in plain language, and summarize **public** information that already lives on the website or in curated public help. It is built for routing, process, and education— not for handling private campaign data.

---

## Where does Kelly start when I open the guide?

Kelly begins from **where you are on the site**. If you share which page you’re on (or pick a topic from the suggestions), answers stay closer to that context. The guide does not know your password, your role in the campaign, or anything not shown on the public site unless you type it.

---

## How do updates to page copy work?

Changing text on a public page (for example a hero line or subtitle) usually follows a steady rhythm:

1. **Edit your draft** — work in the editor while you think it through.
2. **Review the change** — compare what is live to what you’re proposing.
3. **Confirm** — say you’re ready for a final check.
4. **Send update to site** — only this last step saves and swaps what visitors see.

Until that flow finishes successfully, the live page does not change.

---

## What does “Send update to site” mean?

**Send update to site** is the step that **saves your approved wording to the database** so the public site can show it. It is not automatic while you type— you choose when to send. If that step fails, visitors keep seeing the previous version; your draft may still be on your device until you try again or restore a saved draft.

---

## What if my internet drops while I’m editing?

Spotty connection does not publish changes by itself. **Nothing sends without your action.** If you lose connection mid-edit, unfinished work can stay in **this browser** as a draft until you’re back online. If you see a prompt about a recovered draft, you can restore or discard it—that recovery is **only on the device you’re using**, not a cloud backup of campaign systems.

---

## How does beta feedback work?

Notes from the Ask Kelly **beta** go into a **review queue**. Staff may help sort or label items so Kelly and the team can prioritize. **You keep final say** on what you act on. Feedback does **not** silently rewrite the live site— public copy still flows through the normal editor when someone chooses to update it.

---

## What can staff do—and what can’t they?

**Staff can:** help triage feedback, answer tool questions, and support publishing through the same admin paths you use—with permission that matches your role.

**Staff cannot:** pull individual voter rows, donor or treasury records, or private strategy briefs **through the public Ask Kelly box** for you. Those live in separate, access-controlled tools. Ask Kelly on the public site stays within **public-safe** boundaries.

---

## What will Ask Kelly not do?

Ask Kelly will **not**:

- Answer from internal-only manuals or non-public workflows.
- Return voter-file rows, donor histories, bank details, or opposition research.
- Pretend to verify facts it cannot check against public sources you could open yourself.
- Override site permissions— if you don’t have access to an admin screen, the guide can’t unlock it.

---

## Voice assist

Voice assist is **optional**. If read-aloud or speech isn’t available on your device or browser, the site and dashboards still work the usual way—you can always read or type instead.

---

## Safe campaign reports (later)

Over time, **secure campaign tools** may offer **aggregate** reports or simple simulations (totals and summaries). Those belong **outside** the public Ask Kelly box.

The **public-facing Ask Kelly** assistant will **not** expose individual voter rows, donor records, treasury detail, **or** private strategy—even if similar numbers exist elsewhere for staff. Summaries you might see here stay **high-level and public-safe by design.**
