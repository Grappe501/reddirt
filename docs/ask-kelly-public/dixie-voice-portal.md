# Dixie voice portal — policy notes for production

Public-facing onboarding may offer an optional, user-activated spoken capture (“Dixie voice portal”). These rules sit alongside product implementation.

## Voice cloning & likeness

Any custom synthesized voice belonging to or trained on an identifiable person requires **explicit consent from that person** before use in campaign tooling. Synthetic voice output is informational assistance—not permission to authorize financial, legal, or publishing actions.

## Transparency & control inside the organization

Operational voice features should be labeled internally (“beta,” “recording,” machine-assisted readback, etc.) and controlled through the same credentialing you use today for dashboards. Final decisions remain with Kelly and delegated approvers—not with automated playback alone.

## Safety boundaries

Voice output summarizes or reads guidance; **it does not validate compliance** with election law or site policy without human judgment. Playback does not supersede confirmations in editors that actually write public wording.
