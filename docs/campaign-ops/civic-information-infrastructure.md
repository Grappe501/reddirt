# Arkansas civic information infrastructure

Source thread: `https://chatgpt.com/share/69ee423b-470c-83ea-97a2-918ef079e1b5`

Status: future office and campaign communications planning. Treat as a research and product queue, not a claim that current Arkansas systems already provide these features.

## Core pattern

Effective voter education should be:

- Pure voter service, not persuasion
- Good-faith and curiosity-based
- One clear action per post or explainer
- Linked to official sources
- Visually simple and nonpartisan

Working line: trust grows when people understand how systems work.

## "Did You Know Arkansas?" campaign

Recurring short-form voter education series.

Content families:

- Registration deadlines and how to check status
- Early voting windows and location lookup
- Absentee voting rules and deadlines
- Military and overseas voting
- College and first-time voter questions
- Poll worker recruitment and public service
- What the Secretary of State does
- Ballot initiatives and constitutional amendments
- Business filing basics
- Audit process and election certification

## Arkansas election snapshot

Source thread: `https://chatgpt.com/share/69ee4292-d274-83ea-b877-1df89ab15dd4`

Post-election or annual visual civic report. Tone: "Here is how Arkansans showed up."

Possible fields after data/source review:

- Total ballots cast
- Participation by voting method
- First-time voters
- Youth participation
- Rural county participation highlights
- County reporting timelines
- Ballot curing or provisional-ballot education where legally appropriate
- Certification and audit milestones

Purpose:

- Make election administration legible and human
- Use data as reassurance, not intimidation
- Celebrate participation without partisan claims
- Build trust by explaining what happened after polls closed

## Editorial rules

- Use one topic at a time.
- Use official links for final answers.
- Prefer "Did you know?" and "Here is where to check" framing over scolding.
- Avoid candidate imagery and partisan symbols in neutral voter education.
- Flag anything involving legal rights, deadlines, ballot security, or audits for counsel or official-source review.
