## Core insight

Many voters do not know what election audit processes already exist, where reports live, or what audit results mean. Trust improves when election safeguards are visible, searchable, and explained in plain language.

Working line: it is not enough for elections to be secure; people need to understand how they work.

## Information asymmetry frame

Much of the trust gap may come from information asymmetry: election officials understand the system, but voters cannot easily see how it works. This validates public questions without validating misinformation.

Public education should explain:

- Voter roll maintenance
- What triggers voter list changes
- What audits verify
- How voting machines are tested
- Chain of custody
- What safeguards already exist
- Where official procedures live

Working line: sometimes what people call mistrust is really a lack of access to information.

## Public product concepts

- Election Transparency Portal
- Public Audit Dashboard
- Plain-language audit summaries
- Meeting archive with agendas, minutes, videos, and downloadable materials
- Annual Election Trust Report
- Election process explainer videos
- County transparency best-practice toolkit

## Audit education topics

- Difference between an audit and a recount
- What is selected for audit and how selection works
- What voter-verified paper records do
- What audit results can and cannot change
- How certification works
- How vote centers, electronic poll books, paper trails, backups, and tabulation systems differ
- What happens if equipment, internet, or check-in systems fail
- Voter roll maintenance basics under federal and state law
- Official county election procedures manual locations and plain-language summaries

## Messaging guardrails

- Do not use audit language to imply current elections are failing unless primary evidence supports it.
- Do not overpromise unilateral SOS authority.
- Frame around transparency, education, process review, and responsible modernization.
- Keep public tone calm: Arkansas elections can function and still become more transparent.
