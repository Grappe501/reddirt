# Civic participation & direct democracy

## Core principle

**The people of Arkansas should have the final say**—not only on election day, and not only through representatives—**directly, when it matters.**

## Why it matters

People experience decisions as happening *over them* without clear ways to respond. This pillar commits to **structural participation**, not only rhetoric.

## Referendum commitment network (“65,000”)

Build a statewide network of Arkansans who commit to **signing referendum petitions** when legislation crosses a defined line on taking power away from voters—so issues can reach the ballot.

**Not** agreement on every issue—it’s defense of **the right of people to decide.**

At scale, this aims to:

- Signal to the legislature that voters are organized  
- Restore **negotiating power** for the public  
- Enable timely challenges to harmful law  
- Make accountability **structural**

**Strategic line:** *If the people can act, power has to listen.*

## Ballot initiative strategy

Organize coalition-driven initiatives to advance transparency and participation—not only play defense.

### Priority example: dark money / transparency

Coalition work toward:

- Greater transparency in political funding  
- Reducing undisclosed money in Arkansas elections  
- Reinforcing that elections belong to voters, not hidden networks  

Includes: public education, coalition alignment, legal/policy design, signature infrastructure.

### Priority example: youth civic participation

Explore a ballot path to **allow 16- and 17-year-olds to vote in school board elections.**

Rationale (advocacy framing):

- Students are directly affected by board decisions  
- Early participation can build lifelong civic habits  
- Strengthens community engagement  

Implementation notes: study models (e.g. Maryland-style approaches); legal framework; education; school-based engagement; youth leadership.

## Bigger vision

More than single policies—**rebuild a culture of participation:**

- More people in civic life → more responsive government → stronger communities.

## What we’re building (infrastructure)

- Rapid-response referendum network  
- Ballot initiative engine  
- Civic education layer  
- Participation pipeline starting with youth  

**Framing:** people-powered governance—not a campaign-only tactic.

## Messaging anchors (variants)

- *If the people can act, power has to listen.*  
- *We’re not only asking for your vote—we’re building a system where your voice can’t be ignored.*
