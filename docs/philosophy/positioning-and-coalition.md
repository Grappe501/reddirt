# Positioning and coalition

## Movement vs. party (productive frame)

Position as **restoring what the party was supposed to be**—not “fighting Democrats” as the headline.

> We’re not abandoning the party. **We’re restoring what it was supposed to be.**

> We’re **fighting for the people**—and bringing the party back with us.

Framing only as *attacking Democrats* risks **shrinking the coalition** before it forms.

## Emotional anchor (history + identity)

Aim for:

> **A return to something Arkansas once was—not only a rejection of what it is now.**

Strong movements anchor in **identity and history**, not only opposition.

## Homepage / narrative spine (movement layer)

Themes that can sit above candidate-specific pages:

- **“Arkansas wasn’t built by the powerful—it was built by the people.”**
- **New movement** — not outside the party, not controlled by it; for working families, local communities, everyday Arkansans who feel left behind.
- **“Here’s what we heard”** — validated frustration across geography and party ID.
- **Difference** — messaging without action failed; top-down failed; rebuild from the ground up.
- **Beliefs** — power to people; work = stability; local voices; accountability.
- **System, not slogan** — leadership everywhere, trained organizers, feedback loops, long-term power.
- **Turning point** — Arkansas has stood up before; this continues that tradition.
- **CTA** — join, volunteer, community leader pathways; stay connected.

## Name directions (reference list — not final)

Strategic goals for a name: Arkansas-rooted; strength without chaos; breadth beyond hardcore partisans; **people power**.

Examples from brainstorming:

**Strategic shortlist (from prior notes):** Red Dirt Democrats; Front Porch Movement; Arkansas Reclaim—pick based on legal availability, URL, and coalition testing.

## Opposition contrast (when relevant)

Contrast with **status-quo representation** (e.g. institutional continuity vs. people-centered accountability)—use **specific names** only when accurate for the race and compliant with filing/ad rules.
