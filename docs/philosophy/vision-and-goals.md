# Vision and goals

## Campaign vision

**“Power Back to the People — Built Block by Block”**

Government should reflect the **lived reality of everyday Arkansans**—not the priorities of political insiders, corporate donors, or entrenched power networks.

The campaign aims to flip an upside-down system through **structure, participation, and ownership**, not rhetoric alone.

## What this is (meta-frame)

This work is a **prototype for a different kind of political power in Arkansas**—not only a single candidate cycle, though it may be led by a candidate (e.g. Chris Jones in AR-02).

## One-line summaries (pick context-appropriate)

- *“We’re not just running to win an election—we’re building a system where the people of Arkansas never lose their voice again.”*
- *“We’re not just asking for your vote—we’re building a system where your voice can’t be ignored.”*
- *“If the people can act, power has to listen.”* (direct democracy / referendum emphasis)

## Strategic goals

### Short-term

- Win the 2nd Congressional District (when that is the active race).

### Medium-term

- Build a **repeatable model** across Arkansas: school-district-adjacent organizing, coalition-driven campaigns, integrated tech + field infrastructure.

### Long-term

- A **multi-state blueprint** for grassroots democracy: power that is organized, trained, measurable, and **owned by the people**—not rented at election time.

## Underlying thesis

> Political power should be **organized, trained, measurable, and owned by the people**—not rented during election cycles.

The candidate is a **test case**; Arkansas is a **proving ground**.

## Where the theory is strongest (keep investing)

- Structure and systems thinking  
- Integration of tech and field  
- Leadership pipelines  
- Narrative clarity around **who has power**

## Where to sharpen for voters (ongoing)

1. **Translate the system into everyday life** — bills, kids, housing, health.  
2. **Anchor in a small set of crystal-clear issues** even when the philosophy is bigger.  
3. **Keep the person human and visible** — the movement is collective; voters still connect to a person.
