# The People’s Campaign — manifesto

**A manifesto for Arkansas’ 2nd Congressional District — led by Chris Jones**

*(Source narrative for site, print, and speeches—trim per medium.)*

---

## Here’s what we heard

Across Arkansas—porches, church halls, kitchens, classrooms—people are saying the same thing:

They’re working harder than ever… but falling further behind.  
They’re paying more… but getting less.  
They’re speaking up… but not being heard.

We heard from parents, teachers, small business owners, farmers, young people who want to stay but don’t see a future.

Across rural and urban, Black and white, Democrat and Republican—one truth keeps rising:

**The system isn’t built for everyday people anymore.** It’s built for insiders, lobbyists, and those who already have power.

---

## Based on what we heard — this is what we believe

We believe government should work for people who wake up early, work long hours, and still care for family and community.

- **Power should flow from the people** — not only Washington or boardrooms; real power starts in communities and should stay there.
- **Work should mean stability** — afford a home, access care, provide for family, build a future.
- **Representation should be real** — you shouldn’t have to be wealthy or connected to be heard.
- **Opportunity should be local** — communities shouldn’t be drained while wealth concentrates elsewhere.
- **Accountability should be non-negotiable** — elected officials answer to the people every day.

---

## What we’re fighting for

Priorities rooted in real life—not talking points.

1. **Economic dignity for working families** — lower everyday costs; small business and local economies; good jobs; infrastructure that serves communities.  
2. **Healthcare you can count on** — affordable, accessible care; rural providers; protection from crushing medical debt.  
3. **Strong public education** — teachers and classrooms; protect public schools; real workforce pathways.  
4. **Fair representation and voting access** — every vote counts; every community represented; reform systems that silence voices.

---

## What we’re fighting against

- Systems that concentrate power in a few hands  
- Policies that favor corporations over communities  
- Political games that divide while solving nothing  
- Barriers to voting and being heard  

For too long, Arkansas has seen leadership that protects the system instead of the people. **That’s the difference between this campaign and the status quo**—including representation like French Hill’s.

---

## How we get there

We are not only running a campaign—we are **building a system that returns power to people.**

1. **Community-driven organizing** — leadership in every neighborhood, town, county; local ownership, not only asks for votes.  
2. **Leadership at every level** — volunteers → leaders → organizers → community decision-makers; growth after Election Day.  
3. **Grassroots + technology** — conversations, events, digital organizing, timely data and strategy; every conversation and action builds momentum.  
4. **Radical transparency and accountability** — share progress, measure work, stay connected; the campaign belongs to people, not insiders.

---

## The movement we’re building

**Common pain → common purpose → common possibility**

Real change: **from the ground up**, not only top-down.

---

## Our commitment

We are not asking you to believe in one person. We are asking you to believe in **your voice, your community, your power**—and we will reflect that in how we operate.

---

## The choice

Two directions: power stays where it’s always been—or **power returns where it belongs: with the people.**
