# SOS “steward of the public record” — internal messaging & mental model (Pass 1)

**Classification:** **Internal strategy** — not a public web page. **Public** copy must not imply that being **female** (or not male) is a **qualification** for office. Use **values**: clarity, order, service, accuracy, quiet competence.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-27.

---

## 1. Voter mental model (psychology, internal)

- **Intended association:** a **competent steward** of **lists, deadlines, and official documents**—not chaos, not shortcuts. The **librarian** image is a **metaphor** for *organization* and *public service orientation*, not a job title.  
- **Differentiation:** from **partisan brawler** or **insider** — via **process** and **transparency** language (aligned with `keeperOfRecords.ts`).

**Risk:** Any copy that **codes** the office as “women’s work” or **diminishes** the office can backfire. Run **comms** + **legal** on high-reach material.

## 2. Public-safe phrasing (approved directions)

- “**Keeper of the public record**” / “**get the list right**” / “**plain-language** **answers** for **voters and small businesses**.”
- “**Not** the State **Librarian**” — the **SOS and State Library** are **different** roles (see `SOS_AS_KEEPER_OF_RECORDS_MESSAGING.md`).

## 3. Kelly-specific pillars (no identity essentialism)

- **Transparency** that **reduces** the need to **file FOI/FOIA** to learn basic **how and when** for ballot measures and business filings.  
- **Hand-up** for **Ballot Question Committees** and petitioners: **clarity, training, predictable** rules.  
- **Steady** management culture for **county** partners.

## 4. Prohibited in public (examples)

- “**Because** she’s a woman…”  
- “**Unlike** an older man…”
