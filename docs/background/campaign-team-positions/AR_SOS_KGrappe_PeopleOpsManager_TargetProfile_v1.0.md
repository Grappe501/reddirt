Internal Campaign Memo: People Operations Manager – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

Culture is strategy. The People Operations Manager ensures the campaign not only runs, but thrives. This role is about protecting team health, improving communication, and building systems where every team member—paid or volunteer—feels supported, heard, and held to clear expectations.

Role Overview

The People Operations Manager is responsible for onboarding, personnel systems, internal accountability processes, and staff development. They ensure that hiring, staff policies, and internal communications reflect campaign values. They work with the Campaign Manager and Strategic Director to prevent burnout, resolve personnel conflicts, and build a team culture that scales.

Ideal Candidate Profile

Enneagram Type: Type 9 (The Peacemaker) with Wing 1 (The Reformer)- Type 9 Traits: Harmonizing, empathetic, conflict-averse- Wing 1 Traits: Principled, orderly, value-drivenDISC Profile: High S (Steadiness) and High C (Conscientiousness)- High S Traits: Supportive, patient, emotionally steady- High C Traits: Policy-minded, process-oriented, dependableThe ideal candidate is someone who loves making sure others have what they need. They are deeply people-focused, systems-savvy, and able to navigate sensitive interpersonal dynamics with professionalism and discretion.

Preferred Experience

- 3+ years in HR, operations, training, or organizational leadership- Familiarity with labor laws, onboarding/offboarding systems, and team communication tools- Experience writing and implementing team policies or handbooks- Commitment to equity, inclusion, and a collaborative workplace

Campaign Progression Plan

| Phase     | $ Raised       | Role Evolution                                                    ||-----------|----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|| Phase 0   | $0–$100K       | Volunteer – drafts policies, supports onboarding & job postings   || Phase 1   | $100K–$500K    | Paid hourly – runs onboarding, manages hiring materials            || Phase 2   | $500K–$1.25M   | Full-time salaried – leads HR function and culture practices       || Phase 3+  | $1.25M+        | Expands to team development, training systems, and evaluations     |

Compensation Model

- Phase 0: Volunteer- Phase 1: Hourly at discounted 20% rate for >10hrs/week- Phase 2: Full-time salaried (40 hrs/week)- Phase 3+: Bonus eligible and manages internal HR associate if needed

Key Responsibilities by Phase

Phase 0:- Draft internal HR handbook and policies- Build onboarding documents and orientation checklist- Support initial job postings and hiring outreachPhase 1:- Run onboarding sessions and track personnel files- Manage hiring calendar and interview coordination- Start developing internal team communication structurePhase 2:- Lead team check-ins, pulse surveys, and onboarding/offboarding- Develop org chart, workflow documents, and internal expectations guides- Coordinate personnel reviews with Campaign ManagerPhase 3+:- Conduct trainings on culture, boundaries, and DEI practices- Create retention strategy and post-election transition plans- Mediate conflict resolution and personnel escalations

Leadership Progression

The People Operations Manager reports to the Campaign Manager and collaborates across departments. This person becomes the team’s primary advocate and system-builder—ensuring we scale with humanity, clarity, and cohesion. By peak campaign, they will oversee HR systems, support documentation, and team-wide culture strategy.
