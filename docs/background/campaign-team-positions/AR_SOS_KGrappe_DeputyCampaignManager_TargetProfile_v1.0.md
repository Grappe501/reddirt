Internal Campaign Memo: Deputy Campaign Manager – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

As the campaign scales, the Campaign Manager needs a tactical partner—someone who understands strategy, manages people, and solves problems before they become crises. The Deputy Campaign Manager is that partner: detail-minded, decisive, and trusted by every team.

Role Overview

The Deputy Campaign Manager supports the Campaign Manager in overseeing day-to-day campaign execution. They manage key departments (Field, Operations, Volunteer, Events, or Scheduling), troubleshoot logistical and personnel issues, and ensure cross-departmental alignment. They serve as a buffer between strategy and execution—freeing up senior leadership to plan while keeping everything on track.

Leadership Progression

The Deputy Campaign Manager reports directly to the Campaign Manager and Strategic Director. They will serve as the Campaign Manager’s operational right hand and step into full leadership responsibilities as needed throughout the campaign.
