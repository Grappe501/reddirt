Scheduling Assistant – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

Every great campaign is built on clear plans and punctual execution. The Scheduling Assistant helps keep the Candidate’s calendar running smoothly while supporting the day-to-day scheduling needs across the campaign.

Role Overview

The Scheduling Assistant supports the Scheduling Coordinator with internal calendar management, event prep documents, and scheduling logistics. This person plays a key role in organizing candidate availability, prepping for public appearances, and making sure internal and external meetings stay aligned.

Key Responsibilities

- Maintain and update daily calendar requests and meetings- Prepare and distribute briefing materials and daily run-of-show- Coordinate reminders and travel timing with campaign leads- Help track incoming requests for Candidate or department availability- Communicate scheduling updates to internal team promptly

Qualifications

- Prior experience in administration, scheduling, or project coordination- Familiarity with Google Calendar and spreadsheet tools- Organized, responsive, and comfortable with high-pressure coordination- Excellent communication and time management- Prior experience in a campaign, school, or nonprofit environment preferred

Compensation & Progression

This role follows a milestone-based growth plan:| Phase   | $ Raised     | Compensation & Role Evolution                                  ||---------|--------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|| Phase 0 | $0–$100K     | Volunteer – supports calendar maintenance and prep doc creation || Phase 1 | $100K–$500K  | Paid hourly – manages day-to-day tracking and daily briefings    || Phase 2 | $500K–$1.25M | Full-time salaried – supports full calendar flow and tour prep   || Phase 3+| $1.25M+      | May assist with press, event briefings, or travel coordination   |

Leadership Structure

The Scheduling Assistant reports to the Scheduling Coordinator and Operations Director. They help ensure the campaign runs on time and with intention every day, providing clarity and prep support across teams.
