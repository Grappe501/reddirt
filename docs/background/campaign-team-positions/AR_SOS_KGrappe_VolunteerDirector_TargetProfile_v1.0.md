Internal Campaign Memo: Volunteer Director – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

This is a movement fueled by people—not politics as usual. The Volunteer Director is the heartbeat of that movement. They are the architect of how we recruit, train, and activate thousands of supporters across Arkansas. This role blends relational leadership with operational structure, creating systems where volunteers don’t just show up—they grow.

Role Overview

The Volunteer Director is responsible for volunteer recruitment, onboarding, training, tracking, and leadership development. They will build scalable systems that ensure volunteers are informed, supported, and equipped to engage in canvassing, phone banking, voter outreach, events, and more. They will also play a key role in converting volunteers into local precinct captains, organizers, or staff members.

Ideal Candidate Profile

Enneagram Type: Type 2 (The Helper) with Wing 3 (The Achiever)- Type 2 Traits: Empathetic, warm, empowering- Wing 3 Traits: Goal-oriented, driven, upliftingDISC Profile: High I (Influence) and High S (Steadiness)- High I Traits: Motivational, energetic, outgoing- High S Traits: Supportive, reliable, team-orientedThe ideal candidate is someone who thrives on connection, can build community from scratch, and has an instinct for mentorship. They believe people are the strategy and have the systems-mindset to scale that belief into statewide volunteer infrastructure.

Preferred Experience

- 2+ cycles or 3+ years in volunteer management or community organizing- Experience with distributed organizing models and leadership pipelines- Familiarity with tools like VAN, Mobilize, Google Forms, and relational organizing apps- Strong facilitation, communication, and volunteer training skills

Campaign Progression Plan

| Phase     | $ Raised       | Role Evolution                                                    ||-----------|----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|| Phase 0   | $0–$100K       | Volunteer – helps organize events, intake forms, team meetings    || Phase 1   | $100K–$500K    | Paid hourly – leads onboarding, builds tracking + engagement      || Phase 2   | $500K–$1.25M   | Full-time salaried – leads volunteer department, launches captains|| Phase 3+  | $1.25M+        | Manages statewide volunteer teams, leadership dev, and training   |

Compensation Model

- Phase 0: Volunteer- Phase 1: Hourly at discounted 20% rate for >10hrs/week- Phase 2: Full-time salaried (40 hrs/week)- Phase 3+: Eligible for bonus pool and potential for Deputy Directors

Key Responsibilities by Phase

Phase 0:- Create sign-up and intake systems- Help organize first volunteer events and house parties- Coordinate call time support volunteers for fundraisingPhase 1:- Manage onboarding process and engagement flow- Run weekly team meetings and build regional volunteer leads- Track activity and shift coverage using Mobilize and VANPhase 2:- Launch captain and precinct volunteer leader programs- Train volunteers to run house meetings, canvasses, and phonebanks- Coordinate with Ops and Field for event staffing and logisticsPhase 3+:- Build full training library and leadership development track- Integrate volunteer tracking into campaign performance dashboard- Oversee team of regional volunteer managers or coordinators

Leadership Progression

The Volunteer Director reports to the Field Director and Campaign Manager, and works in close collaboration with Digital, Events, and Operations. By peak campaign season, this role becomes one of the most public-facing and widely connected positions on staff—representing our belief that everyday Arkansans are the best organizers we have.
