Internal Campaign Memo: Events Director – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

In a grassroots movement like ours, events are the front line of trust, inspiration, and action. The Events Director is responsible for making sure every town hall, campaign stop, fundraiser, and field event delivers impact, organization, and energy. This is not just about logistics—it’s about turning moments into momentum.

Role Overview

The Events Director oversees planning, logistics, staffing, and execution of all public campaign events. They ensure that events are aligned with campaign goals, staffed with trained volunteers, and logistically sound. They work closely with the Scheduling Coordinator, Field Director, Volunteer Director, and Communications team to ensure events are on-message, well-promoted, and effectively executed.

Ideal Candidate Profile

Enneagram Type: Type 7 (The Enthusiast) with Wing 6 (The Loyalist)- Type 7 Traits: Energetic, fast-moving, adaptable, crowd-oriented- Wing 6 Traits: Loyal, planner, detail-consciousDISC Profile: High I (Influence) and High C (Conscientiousness)- High I Traits: Outgoing, collaborative, people-person- High C Traits: Planning-focused, precise, logistics-mindedThe ideal Events Director is someone who can see both the big picture and the tiniest logistical details. They know how to make a gym feel like a movement, how to manage staff and volunteers gracefully, and how to deliver both emotional resonance and clockwork precision.

Preferred Experience

- 2+ cycles or 3+ years planning political, nonprofit, or large-scale public events- Experience with field event logistics, staging, and AV coordination- Familiarity with field offices, candidate travel, press setup, and event compliance- Strong organizational, time-management, and cross-team coordination skills

Campaign Progression Plan

Compensation Model

- Phase 0: Volunteer- Phase 1: Hourly at discounted 20% rate for >10hrs/week- Phase 2: Full-time salaried (40 hrs/week)- Phase 3+: Event support team or regional advance staff may be added

Key Responsibilities by Phase

Phase 0:- Scout locations and help set up town halls and house parties- Begin building event checklists and day-of proceduresPhase 1:- Manage vendor relationships, AV setup, signage, and event flow- Coordinate with Volunteer and Scheduling teams for staffing and timing- Create and distribute run-of-show documents for all major eventsPhase 2:- Advance all events and manage on-site execution- Coordinate surrogates and press when needed- Maintain archive of past events and event reporting metricsPhase 3+:- Manage full slate of weekly rallies, community meetings, and rapid-response events- Train advance staff and deploy regional event support- Lead cross-department event planning meetings

Leadership Progression

The Events Director reports to the Field Director and Campaign Manager. This role collaborates across Scheduling, Comms, Digital, and Volunteer to create high-quality experiences that grow the campaign, energize supporters, and move media narratives. By peak season, the Events Director may oversee multiple staff or regional leads.
