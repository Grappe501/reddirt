Campaign Manager – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

The Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State campaign is a mission-driven movement emphasizing trust, momentum, local leadership, and adaptability. Every team member is expected to operate as part of a cohesive, mission-focused team. We are not merely hiring for jobs; we are inviting individuals into a strategy built on shared values and collective goals.All team members initially wear multiple hats, with duties evolving as we achieve funding milestones and bring on new personnel. The Campaign Manager plays a pivotal role in this structure, requiring flexibility, resilience, and a commitment to the campaign's overarching mission.

Role Overview

The Campaign Manager is the operational linchpin of the campaign, responsible for executing the strategic vision set forth by the Strategic Director and the Candidate. This role encompasses the management of daily campaign activities, coordination of staff and volunteers, oversight of fundraising efforts, and ensuring effective communication both internally and externally.

Key Responsibilities

- Strategic Planning: Collaborate with the Strategic Director to develop and implement comprehensive campaign strategies.- Team Management: Recruit, train, and supervise campaign staff and volunteers, fostering a collaborative and efficient work environment.- Fundraising Oversight: Lead fundraising initiatives, including donor outreach, event planning, and compliance with campaign finance laws.- Budget Management: Develop and manage the campaign budget, ensuring fiscal responsibility and transparency.- Communications Coordination: Oversee the development and dissemination of campaign messaging across various platforms.- Event Planning: Organize and manage campaign events, ensuring alignment with strategic objectives.- Data Analysis: Utilize data analytics to inform decision-making and optimize campaign performance.

Qualifications

- Proven experience in political campaign management or related fields.- Strong leadership and organizational skills.- Excellent communication and interpersonal abilities.- Proficiency in campaign management tools and data analysis.- Commitment to the campaign's mission and values.

Compensation & Progression

This position follows a phase-based growth model:| Phase | $ Raised       | Compensation & Role Evolution ||-------|----------------|------------------------------|| Phase 0 | $0–$100K       | Volunteer role encompassing multiple responsibilities including field ops      || Phase 1 | $100K–$500K    | Transition to paid CM role, overseeing daily operations and coordination       || Phase 2 | $500K–$1.25M   | Expansion of team; CM focuses on strategy implementation and oversight          || Phase 3+ | $1.25M+        | Full-scale campaign operations; CM leads a comprehensive team across departments|

Leadership Structure

The Campaign Manager reports directly to the Strategic Director and, by extension, the Candidate. As the campaign scales, this role evolves to encompass greater strategic responsibilities, including mentorship of junior staff and representation of the campaign in high-stakes environments.
