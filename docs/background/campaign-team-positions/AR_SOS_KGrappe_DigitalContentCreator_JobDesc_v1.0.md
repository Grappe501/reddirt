Digital Content Creator – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

We believe storytelling is organizing. The Digital Content Creator helps us inspire, inform, and engage voters across Arkansas through compelling visual and narrative content.

Role Overview

The Digital Content Creator produces digital assets including graphics, videos, and written content for use across social media, email, SMS, and digital ads. This position plays a key role in brand consistency, supporter engagement, and fast-turnaround storytelling moments.

Key Responsibilities

- Shoot, edit, and produce short-form videos for social media platforms- Design campaign graphics that reflect the brand voice and visual identity- Assist in writing captions, emails, and digital copy tied to campaign events and issues- Maintain an organized asset library and archive system- Support the Digital Director with day-to-day content creation and rapid response needs

Qualifications

- 2+ years experience in digital content creation or design- Proficiency in Canva, Adobe Creative Suite, CapCut, or similar tools- Experience managing content for Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, TikTok, and YouTube- Strong attention to detail and creative instincts- Bonus: political, campaign, or movement storytelling background

Compensation & Progression

This role follows a milestone-based growth model:| Phase   | $ Raised     | Compensation & Role Evolution                                   ||---------|--------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|| Phase 0 | $0–$100K     | Volunteer – supports graphic design and simple editing            || Phase 1 | $100K–$500K  | Paid hourly – manages core visual content and daily social media  || Phase 2 | $500K–$1.25M | Full-time salaried – leads brand identity and video storytelling  || Phase 3+| $1.25M+      | Oversees content calendar and contracts other creative producers  |

Leadership Structure

The Digital Content Creator reports to the Digital Director and collaborates with the Communications, Field, and Events teams. This position is responsible for bringing the campaign’s story to life visually and emotionally online.
