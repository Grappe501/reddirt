Internal Campaign Memo: Digital Director – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

The Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State campaign is powered by people, data, and story. The Digital Director transforms our values and vision into compelling, strategic, and measurable digital action. This is a role for someone who understands narrative, moves fast, and brings both creative flair and analytical discipline. Our digital presence is not a side function—it’s the front line of persuasion, mobilization, and fundraising.

Role Overview

The Digital Director leads all aspects of digital communication, organizing, and fundraising. This includes email, SMS, social media, digital ads, content strategy, and online volunteer recruitment. They work closely with the Communications Director to ensure message consistency and collaborate with Finance to maximize ROI on digital channels. This person must understand both the culture and the code of digital organizing.

Ideal Candidate Profile

Enneagram Type: Type 3 (The Achiever) with Wing 4 (The Creative)- Type 3 Traits: Strategic, adaptable, goal-oriented- Wing 4 Traits: Creative, insightful, mission-drivenDISC Profile: High I (Influence) and High C (Conscientiousness)- High I Traits: Engaging, expressive, persuasive- High C Traits: Structured, results-focused, content-awareThe ideal candidate is equal parts content creator and data geek. They know how to write for conversion, test for results, and build systems that scale. They thrive on iteration, teamwork, and driving engagement that converts clicks into community power.

Preferred Experience

- 2+ cycles in digital roles, ideally as Digital Director or senior digital strategist- Deep experience with platforms: ActBlue, NGP, Mobilize, ActionNetwork, Spoke, or similar- Track record growing email and SMS lists and converting them to fundraising and field power- Proficiency in social media planning, digital ads, peer-to-peer texting- Understanding of A/B testing, conversion tracking, and digital analytics

Campaign Progression Plan

| Phase     | $ Raised       | Role Evolution                                                  ||-----------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|| Phase 0   | $0–$100K       | Volunteer support: email drafts, social media posts             || Phase 1   | $100K–$500K    | Paid hourly; manages email, SMS, content calendar               || Phase 2   | $500K–$1.25M   | Full-time salaried; oversees strategy, list-building, engagement|| Phase 3+  | $1.25M+        | Leads department with contractors or staff for ads, content, CRM|

Compensation Model

- Phase 0: Volunteer- Phase 1: Hourly at discounted 20% rate for >10hrs/week- Phase 2: Full-time salaried (40 hrs/week)- Phase 3+: Bonus eligible; can add direct reports or consultants

Key Responsibilities by Phase

Phase 0:- Build initial email content and SMS templates- Design organic social graphics and outreach language- Audit current platforms and recommend digital stackPhase 1:- Manage regular outbound email/SMS with testing and analytics- Coordinate digital launch events and social rollouts- Grow supporter list and deepen segmentationPhase 2:- Build and manage online fundraising and organizing calendar- Plan and launch persuasion and mobilization ads (in-house or vendor-led)- Coordinate with field, finance, and comms to ensure unified voice and timingPhase 3+:- Supervise content creation (video, email, SMS, ads)- Integrate all digital metrics into central campaign dashboard- Adapt digital strategy weekly based on analytics and real-world response

Leadership Progression

The Digital Director reports to the Communications Director and Campaign Manager, while maintaining strong alignment with the Finance and Field teams. They will have increasing authority over budget, vendors, and content strategy. By peak campaign season, this position will direct multiple contractors or team members, own digital budget, and ensure digital performance meets or exceeds KPI targets.
