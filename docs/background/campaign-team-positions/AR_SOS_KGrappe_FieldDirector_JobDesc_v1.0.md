Field Director – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

The Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State campaign is a mission-driven movement emphasizing trust, momentum, local leadership, and adaptability. Every team member operates as part of a cohesive, mission-focused team. We are not merely hiring for jobs; we are inviting individuals into a strategy built on shared values and collective goals.All team members initially wear multiple hats, with duties evolving as we achieve funding milestones and expand our personnel. The Field Director plays a pivotal role in this structure, requiring flexibility, resilience, and a commitment to the campaign's overarching mission.

Role Overview

The Field Director is responsible for developing and executing the campaign's grassroots organizing strategy. This includes managing field staff and volunteers, coordinating voter outreach efforts, and ensuring effective community engagement. The Field Director works closely with the Campaign Manager and Strategic Director to align field operations with the overall campaign strategy.

Key Responsibilities

- Strategic Planning: Collaborate with campaign leadership to develop and implement comprehensive field strategies.- Team Management: Recruit, train, and supervise field staff and volunteers, fostering a collaborative and efficient work environment.- Voter Outreach: Oversee voter contact programs, including canvassing, phone banking, and community events.- Data Analysis: Utilize data analytics to inform decision-making and optimize field operations.- Community Engagement: Build and maintain relationships with community leaders and organizations to enhance campaign visibility and support.- Compliance: Ensure all field activities comply with campaign finance laws and regulations.

Qualifications

- Proven experience in political campaign field operations or related fields.- Strong leadership and organizational skills.- Excellent communication and interpersonal abilities.- Proficiency in campaign management tools and data analysis.- Commitment to the campaign's mission and values.

Compensation & Progression

This position follows a phase-based growth model:| Phase   | $ Raised      | Compensation & Role Evolution                                                        ||---------|---------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|| Phase 0 | $0–$100K      | Volunteer role encompassing multiple responsibilities including field ops             || Phase 1 | $100K–$500K   | Transition to paid Field Director role, overseeing daily field operations             || Phase 2 | $500K–$1.25M  | Expansion of team; Field Director focuses on higher-level strategy implementation     || Phase 3+| $1.25M+       | Full-scale campaign operations; Field Director leads comprehensive field team         |

Leadership Structure

The Field Director will report directly to the Campaign Manager and, by extension, the Strategic Director. As the campaign scales, this role evolves to encompass greater strategic responsibilities, including mentorship of junior staff and representation of the campaign in high-stakes environments.
