Internal Campaign Memo: Operations Director – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Campaign Philosophy & Culture

The Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State campaign is a fast-moving, people-powered operation rooted in strategy, adaptability, and mission-aligned leadership. The Operations Director is the glue that holds the machine together—building systems that scale, solving problems before they escalate, and supporting the team with tools and resources that make our impact possible. This is a role for someone who thrives behind the scenes but whose fingerprints are on every success.This role evolves with campaign milestones. At the start, the Operations Director will handle scheduling, logistics, HR, tech, and physical space management. By Phase 3, this position will oversee department heads across compliance, data, internal systems, and scheduling.

Role Overview

The Operations Director is responsible for campaign infrastructure, internal coordination, team support, vendor management, and logistics. This includes managing the master campaign calendar, staffing logistics for events, onboarding new hires and volunteers, managing software and hardware tools, and ensuring the entire organization runs efficiently and with accountability.

Leadership Progression

The Operations Director reports to the Campaign Manager and Strategic Director. As the campaign scales, this role becomes the nerve center of campaign execution—ensuring every plan can be implemented with excellence. The Director will supervise 3–5 team members by peak season and serve as internal lead for scheduling, field support, and department-wide communication.
