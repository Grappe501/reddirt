Volunteer Role Descriptions – Kelly Grappe for Secretary of State

Precinct Captain

Precinct Captains serve as neighborhood organizers, leading local voter outreach and community visibility. They are the main point of contact for their precinct and help build a hyperlocal volunteer base.

Core Responsibilities:

• Host house parties and canvassing days in their precinct.

• Recruit and manage volunteers in their neighborhood.

• Track yard sign needs, voter questions, and community issues.

• Report weekly to the Field or Volunteer Director.

Phone Bank Captain

Phone Bank Captains lead weekly remote or in-person phone banks. They help onboard new callers, ensure accuracy in scripts, and maintain call energy and data integrity.

Core Responsibilities:

• Facilitate 1–2 phone banks per week.

• Train new volunteers and provide live support.

• Track performance and submit results to Volunteer Director.

• Help adapt scripts based on feedback.

Canvass Lead / Staging Location Lead

Canvass Leads are responsible for organizing, launching, and debriefing door-to-door canvasses. They run staging locations and ensure volunteers have materials, support, and motivation.

Core Responsibilities:

• Manage check-in, packet distribution, and turf assignments.

• Oversee walk list returns and data entry tracking.

• Train new canvassers and troubleshoot questions.

• Submit detailed event reports to Field Team.

Volunteer Recruitment Captain

These volunteers focus on growing the volunteer base by conducting outreach in their communities. They identify new supporters and plug them into events and trainings.

Core Responsibilities:

• Table or flyer at local events, churches, and schools.

• Refer 5+ new volunteers per week to Volunteer Director.

• Follow up with inactive or one-time volunteers.

• Assist with new volunteer onboarding calls or texts.

Community Ambassador / Liaison

Community Ambassadors represent the campaign in targeted communities (faith, education, veterans, youth, etc.). They act as a listening post and local advocate.

Core Responsibilities:

• Organize or co-host events in your community circle.

• Report community concerns or feedback to leadership.

• Distribute campaign materials within your network.

• Promote campaign visibility and voter education.

Data Entry Volunteer

These volunteers support digital infrastructure by entering voter contact data, event RSVPs, and list corrections. Accuracy and confidentiality are essential.

Core Responsibilities:

• Enter contact results into VAN or provided systems.

• Maintain event attendance logs and sign-ins.

• Flag duplicate or invalid entries.

• Work with Data Manager to update lists weekly.

Event Support Volunteer

These volunteers are the face of the campaign at events—setting up, welcoming guests, and managing materials.

Core Responsibilities:

• Assist with event setup, signage, and teardown.

• Greet and sign in attendees.

• Manage literature, swag, and crowd flow.

• Debrief event logistics with Event Lead.

Social Media Amplifier

Amplifiers help spread the campaign’s message by engaging with content, boosting posts, and promoting events in local networks.

Core Responsibilities:

• Share and repost campaign social content weekly.

• Tag friends and promote volunteer opportunities.

• Post original content (photos/videos) from events.

• Monitor and flag online engagement issues.

Driver / Logistics Volunteer

Drivers help move materials, deliver yard signs, or transport the Candidate/team across counties. This role is crucial for rural and high-volume campaign days.

Core Responsibilities:

• Transport volunteers, staff, or materials as scheduled.

• Assist with signage delivery or large pickups.

• Maintain a clean driving record and reliable vehicle.

• Communicate route changes or delays with Ops Team.

Faith or School Outreach Volunteer

This role builds campaign visibility and trust in schools, congregations, and youth-led spaces. It focuses on voter education and volunteer recruitment through trusted relationships.

Core Responsibilities:

• Deliver voter resources to school and church networks.

• Speak briefly at community events or services.

• Host listening sessions or volunteer orientation meetups.

• Report back on institutional priorities and voter concerns.
